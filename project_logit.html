Methodology

Data cleaning and transformation

The work starts by connecting to a data source. It could be a database, Excel file, web service, or other platforms. After selecting relevant columns and tables, exploration of the imported data follows to understand its structure, relationships, and quality. This involves checking data types, identifying missing values and understanding the distribution of data. Corrections are made at this stage to ensure optimal data quality for future loads.

If necessary, additional actions take place here, such as filtering rows, renaming columns or enriching the data by creating calculated columns.

Semantic model design

Power BI relies on accurate information about table relationships to ensure the seamless functioning of visuals and the correct application of filters with respect to the logic of the data. As you can see, the model follows a star schema, which is considered best practice. Notice the Date table in the bottom right corner. It is a calculated table necessary for the time dimension to work in visuals. Although Power BI is able to manage dates on its own, it is a matter of optimal performance to do it manually.
